Fast Subsidies, Furious Emissions: The Double-Edged Impact of EV Policies

University of Maryland

Examined the environmental impact of electric vehicle subsidies in India, focusing on pollution substitution effects through increased electricity demand in a fossil-fuel-dominated grid.

// Social Welfare Optimization
Objective: minₚ,ₛ V(p) + G(r(s,c)) + O
Constraints:
  ∫y(e(p),r(s,c),w)dw ≤ ȳ  (Production)
  B ≥ s + p              (Budget)

Key Findings:
• EV subsidies alone increase grid emissions
• Solar microgrid subsidies mitigate this effect
• Coordinated policies maximize welfare gains

Key Empirical Results

+576 EVs per 1% increase in electricity generation
-4.7 μg/m³ SO₂ reduction post-FAME policy
